About Roger Sutherland

Roger C. Sutherland is a Professional Engineer and a principal water resources engineer with more than five decades of experience in watershed planning, urban hydrology, stormwater quality modeling, and pollutant reduction strategies.

His work focuses on understanding and quantifying the processes that control urban runoff pollutant generation, transport, and removal. Over the course of his career he has applied hydrologic, hydraulic, and water quality modeling techniques to evaluate continuous runoff flows, sediment transport, and associated pollutant loadings from urban landscapes. His analyses have supported watershed management plans, TMDL development, stormwater master planning, and the design and evaluation of best management practices across North America and internationally.

Mr. Sutherland is the principal author of the Simplified Particulate Transport Model (SIMPTM), a stormwater quality modeling framework developed to simulate long-term pollutant load generation and the effectiveness of maintenance practices such as street sweeping and catch basin cleaning. The model has been applied to numerous watershed and regulatory studies and was recognized by the U.S. Geological Survey in the 1990s as one of the most advanced stormwater quality modeling tools available.

Since beginning his research at the University of Maryland in the early 1970s, he has contributed to many of the foundational studies on urban runoff and nonpoint source pollution, including projects associated with the Clean Water Act Section 208 planning efforts and the Nationwide Urban Runoff Program (NURP). His professional work has supported municipalities, transportation agencies, and watershed authorities in evaluating practical stormwater management strategies that emphasize source control and cost-effective pollutant reduction.

Mr. Sutherland has authored more than fifty technical publications on stormwater quality modeling and the benefits of public works maintenance practices. He has presented or taught at conferences, workshops, and professional training programs more than one hundred times since 1975 and has served as an instructor for numerous professional development courses focused on stormwater modeling, BMP design, and urban runoff management.
